Halloween

History

1. What is the origin of Halloween?
Halloween originated from the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain.
2. Why do people carve pumpkins on Halloween?
Carving pumpkins comes from the Irish legend of Jack O'Lantern.
3. Where does the word "Halloween" come from?
The word "Halloween" comes from "All Hallows' Eve", the night before All Saints' Day.
4. How was Halloween introduced to the United States?
Irish and Scottish immigrants brought Halloween to the U.S. in the 19th century.
5. What was the original purpose of Halloween costumes?
Costumes were used to disguise people from evil spirits during Samhain.
6. Why is Halloween celebrated on October 31st?
Halloween is on October 31st because of its association with the Celtic festival of Samhain.

Traditions

7. What does trick-or-treating mean?
Trick-or-treating is when children ask for candy, promising not to play tricks in return.
8. Why do people wear masks on Halloween?
Masks were worn to protect from evil spirits during ancient celebrations.
9. What do bonfires symbolize during Halloween?
Bonfires were lit to protect people from spirits and to celebrate Samhain.
10. How is Halloween celebrated in the U.S.?
In the U.S., Halloween is celebrated with trick-or-treating, costumes, and parties.
11. What is the meaning of orange and black during Halloween?
Orange represents harvest, and black symbolizes death and darkness.
12. What is the origin of "trick-or-treat"?
"Trick-or-treat" has roots in medieval traditions of asking for food in exchange for prayers.
